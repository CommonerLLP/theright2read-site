How this is made

The Right to Read is a social movement committed to realizing Ambedkar's Constitution, and we are open about how our work is produced.

The words and the analysis are social.

Every argument, every history, every reading of the evidence, every working paper is written and reasoned by people. We do not use AI to generate our text or our analysis. Where a date, a number, or a legal claim appears, a person has verified it against a primary source.

The logo is the work of a human designer.

AI helps us build, not think.

Our use of AI is concentrated on the engineering — writing and reviewing Python and JavaScript, processing data, and building and testing this website with its charts and maps. Campaign engineers use a combination of large-language-model tools and agentic workflows for this, and they direct, review, and correct everything those tools produce.

People are accountable.

Whatever tool touched a file, the people of the campaign take full responsibility for every claim, figure, map, and line of code we publish. Mistakes are ours to own and to correct — tell us at jaibhim [at] theright2read [dot] org.
