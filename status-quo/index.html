What India spends on libraries

India spends about ₹4.77 per person per year on public libraries, states and the Centre together, in real terms. The best-funded state spends roughly 1,400× the worst, both under the same Constitution. Here is the money, in full.

State & UT · 2020-21

Every State. One scandal.

Per-capita public-library spending, nominal ₹ per person per year, latest year reported — all 28 States, plus the two Union Territories with an elected legislature (Delhi, Puducherry). Jharkhand, at the top, spends 10 paise; Goa, at the bottom, spends about ₹140. This is the raw spread; the ₹4.77 headline above is the real-terms national average, States and Centre combined.

Source: Ministry of Culture, state-wise per-capita library spending, 2014-15 to 2020-21 (nominal ₹).

Covers the 28 States and the two UTs with an elected legislature (Delhi, Puducherry). Excludes the five UTs with no legislature and no separate library-spend line — Andaman & Nicobar, Chandigarh, Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu, Lakshadweep, and Ladakh. Jammu & Kashmir is treated separately: its pre-2019 figures cannot be split from Ladakh, and we report none of this as endorsement of the status the Indian state asserts over the region, or of the 2019 removal of its statehood.

Methodology

How these numbers are built.

Per-capita figures come from the CAG Combined Finance & Revenue Accounts (Major Head 2205, sub-head 105) and the Ministry of Culture's state-wise series, 2014-15 to 2020-21. The ₹4.77 hero figure is the consolidated state-plus-Centre series (state spending plus the Centre's RRRLF grant), in constant 2011-12 ₹ via the MoSPI implicit GDP deflator; the real-terms peak was ₹8.17 in 2018-19. Comparators (statue, corporate tax cut) are from the Government of Gujarat (2018) and CAG / Finance Ministry annexures (FY 2019-20). A fuller per-state write-up is in preparation.
